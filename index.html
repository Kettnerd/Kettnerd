01 — Research

What Keeps Me Up at Night

When does a model learn to be biased? Can we intervene at the moment it happens? These aren't just academic questions — they shape who gets heard, who gets hired, and who gets helped.

I believe fairness shouldn't be an afterthought. If we can understand exactly when and how bias crystallizes during training, we can build systems that are equitable from their very first gradient update.

— guiding principle

Bias Emergence in LLMs via Grokking

Investigating when bias crystallizes during LLM training, using the "grokking" phenomenon as a lens to study bias emergence. Exploring whether Chain of Thought techniques can reduce bias through the bias-variance tradeoff framework.

Bias Mitigation LLMs Grokking Chain of Thought Fairness

Training for Objectivity from Initialization

Building a tiny LLM from scratch to test a fundamental question: can we train for objectivity from the very start, rather than correcting bias after the fact? Custom C++/CUDA implementation for fine-grained control over every stage of the training process.

C++ CUDA nanoGPT HPC / ROSIE AI Ethics
